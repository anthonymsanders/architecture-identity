# OpenText Identity Management (IDM) Capabilities

## Overview
OpenText Identity Management (IDM) provides enterprise-grade solutions for managing digital identities, access, and compliance across complex environments, including higher education and government.

## Core Capabilities
- **Identity Lifecycle Management:**
  - Automated provisioning and deprovisioning of user accounts
  - Role-based access control
  - Self-service password and profile management
- **Authentication & Single Sign-On (SSO):**
  - Supports SSO across multiple applications
  - Multi-factor authentication (MFA) integration
  - Centralized authentication for web, cloud, and on-premises systems
- **Directory Services:**
  - Integration with LDAP, Active Directory, and other directory platforms
  - Centralized identity repository
- **Federation & External Access:**
  - SAML, OAuth, OpenID Connect support for federated identity
  - Enables secure access to partner and cloud services
- **Access Governance:**
  - Policy enforcement and access reviews
  - Compliance reporting and audit trails
  - Segregation of duties and risk management
- **Privileged Access Management:**
    - Controls and monitors privileged accounts
    - Session recording and real-time alerts
    - OpenText IDM supports Privileged Access Management (PAM), including session monitoring, real-time alerts, and privileged account controls. For PAM features, the typical license SKU required is the Privileged Access SKU. See [OpenText IAM Solutions](https://www.opentext.com/products/identity-access-management) and [OpenText Support Resources](https://www.opentext.com/support/resources) for details and setup guidance.
 
  - APIs for custom integrations
  - Connectors for Microsoft, SAP, Oracle, and other platforms
  - Supports GDPR, UK Data Protection Act, and other regulatory requirements
  - Audit logging and reporting

## Use Cases in Higher Education

- Secure access to campus systems and resources
